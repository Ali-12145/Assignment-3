Whittier

Whittier, Alaska, is a unique town where nearly all residents live in one building. Known for its beautiful scenery and outdoor activities, Whittier offers a unique glimpse into Alaskan life.Whittier is located in Los Angeles County, about 12 miles southeast of the City of Los Angeles. A five Member City Council under the Council-Manager form of government directs the City. Whittier is a charter law city and was incorporated in 1898. The Charter form of City government was ratified in 1955. The City covers 14.8 square miles and has an estimated population of 87,369 as of January 1, 2018. Businesses and industries in the area include 436 professional services, 845 retail stores, 200 family-type restaurants, 37 manufacturing plants, 7 hotels and motels, 2 new automobile dealerships and over 303 specialty shops and boutiques, predominantly located in Uptown Whittier, the Quad shopping mall, as well as the Whittwood Town Center

Wasilla

Wasilla, located in the Matanuska-Susitna Valley of Alaska, is known for its stunning natural beauty. The city serves as a gateway to outdoor adventures, including fishing, hiking, and skiing.ake Lucile Park is nestled in a heavily wooded forest of birch, spruce and cottonwood trees crossing through a gentle terrain of open fields which offers many outdoor opportunities. Enjoy the amenities of three pavilions, a playground, three athletic fields, large parking areas, picnic tables, and public toilet facilities. The 57 campsites provide limited accommodation for motor homes and tents during the summer months. Also available is the large group encampment with the larger pavilion which is accessible by the Lake Lucile Park trail system.
